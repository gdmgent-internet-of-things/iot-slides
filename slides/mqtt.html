MQTT

  • Message Queuing: Messages are queued and delivered asynchronously, allowing for decoupling of communication.
  • Telemetry: Particularly suited for telemetry and remote monitoring applications, where data is collected from sensors and transmitted efficiently.
  • Transport: Provides a lightweight transport mechanism for sending and receiving messages over various networks.

MQTT is a lightweight messaging protocol for small sensors and mobile devices, designed to be efficient, flexible, and reliable.

What MQTT Does

Pub/Sub Architecture

Allows for message publication and subscription, decoupling senders and receivers.

Quality of Service (QoS)

Supports three levels of QoS to ensure message delivery.

More info
QoS 0 (At most once): Messages are delivered at most once, with no confirmation or retransmission.
QoS 1 (At least once): Messages are delivered at least once, ensuring no message loss but allowing duplicates.
QoS 2 (Exactly once): Messages are delivered exactly once, guaranteeing no duplicates or message loss.

Low Bandwidth and Overhead

Optimized for low-bandwidth, high-latency, or unreliable networks.

Lightweight

Minimal code footprint, making it suitable for resource-constrained devices.

Where MQTT is Used

Internet of Things (IoT) Deployments

Example: Smart home systems where sensors, actuators, and controllers communicate with each other to automate tasks such as lighting control, temperature regulation, and security monitoring.

M2M (Machine-to-Machine) Communication

Example: Manufacturing plants employing automated machinery that communicate with each other to coordinate production processes, monitor equipment status, and optimize workflow.

Telemetry and Remote Monitoring

Example: Environmental monitoring stations deployed in remote locations, collecting data on air quality, weather conditions, and soil moisture levels, and transmitting this data to centralized servers for analysis.

Real-time Messaging and Notifications

Example: Instant messaging platforms that deliver messages to users in real-time, allowing individuals and groups to communicate seamlessly over the internet.

Popular MQTT Brokers

Mosquitto: An open-source MQTT broker developed by the Eclipse Foundation.
EMQ X: A distributed MQTT broker for large-scale deployments with high availability and performance.
HiveMQ: A scalable and secure MQTT broker suitable for enterprise use cases.
RabbitMQ: A message broker that supports multiple protocols, including MQTT, for message queuing and routing.
Mosca: A Node.js-based MQTT broker that can be easily integrated
